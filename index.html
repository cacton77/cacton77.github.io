Hello world.

My name is Colin. Welcome to my homepage! I am currently a graduate research assistant in the Mechatronics, Automation, and Control Systems (MACS) Laboratory at the University of Washington. My research as a master's student has focused on designing and building mechatronic systems and intelligent control solutions for robotic applications in manufacturing.


Over the last year and a half I have led a team of graduate researchers in the development of a robotic visual inspection platform consisting of a custom, high-resolution imaging end-of-arm-tool, software tools for camera and lighting control, shooting pose traversal policy optimization to reduce inspection time, model-predictive control of illuminance distribution upon the inspected surface, and artificial neural networks for defect identification. This project has progressed under the auspices of the Advanced Robotics for Manufacturing (ARM) Institute, and is currently being deployed for ARM's Datafoundry initiative. Learn more about this and other projects in my portfolio!
